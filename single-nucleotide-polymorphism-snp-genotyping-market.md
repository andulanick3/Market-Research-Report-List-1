<p><p>The Single Nucleotide Polymorphism (SNP) genotyping market is primarily categorized into two types: transitions and transversions. Transitions involve a change between two purines (A to G) or two pyrimidines (C to T), while transversions refer to changes between a purine and a pyrimidine (A to C, A to T, G to C, G to T, C to A, C to G, T to A, T to G). These categories are significant in genetic research, influencing mutation analysis and disease association studies.</p></p>
