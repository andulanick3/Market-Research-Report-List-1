<p><p>The peripheral bone densitometer market comprises several types, including X-Ray Absorptiometry, Ultrasound Bone Densitometers, and other modalities. X-Ray Absorptiometry, the most common type, measures bone density using X-rays to assess risk of osteoporosis. Ultrasound Bone Densitometers utilize sound waves to evaluate bone quality without radiation exposure. Other types may include magnetic resonance imaging (MRI) and quantitative computed tomography (QCT). Each method varies in accuracy, cost, and clinical application, catering to diverse patient needs in bone health assessment.</p></p>
