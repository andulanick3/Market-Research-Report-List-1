<p><p>The polio vaccines market includes two main types: Inactivated Polio Vaccine (IPV) and Oral Polio Vaccine (OPV). IPV is an injectable vaccine containing killed viruses, providing immunity without causing disease, and is primarily used in developed countries. In contrast, OPV is an oral vaccine made from weakened live viruses, which is easier to administer and effective in mass immunization campaigns, particularly in developing regions. The choice between IPV and OPV depends on factors such as accessibility, cost, and epidemiological context.</p></p>
