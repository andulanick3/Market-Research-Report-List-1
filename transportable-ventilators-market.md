<p><p>The transportable ventilators market is segmented into invasive and non-invasive ventilation types. Invasive ventilation involves the use of endotracheal tubes to deliver air directly to the lungs, typically for critically ill patients requiring intensive respiratory support. Non-invasive ventilation, on the other hand, employs masks or nasal devices to assist breathing without requiring intubation. Both types offer flexibility in emergency medical situations, enabling healthcare providers to deliver necessary respiratory support in various settings, including ambulances and remote locations.</p></p>
