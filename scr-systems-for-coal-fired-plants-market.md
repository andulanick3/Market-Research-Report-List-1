<p><p>Selective Catalytic Reduction (SCR) systems are critical technologies for reducing nitrogen oxides (NOx) emissions from coal-fired power plants. By injecting a reductant, typically ammonia, into the flue gas stream, SCR systems facilitate a chemical reaction that converts NOx into nitrogen and water, thus mitigating environmental impact. The increasing regulatory focus on air quality and emissions reduction is driving the adoption of these systems across the globe.</p><p>The SCR systems for coal-fired plants market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 11.1% during the forecast period, fueled by advancements in SCR technology and the rising pressure on utilities to comply with stringent emission standards. <p><p>SCR (Selective Catalytic Reduction) systems for coal-fired plants are designed to reduce nitrogen oxide (NOx) emissions. Anhydrous ammonia SCR systems inject dry ammonia gas into the exhaust stream, achieving high efficiency and low emissions. Aqueous ammonia SCR systems use diluted ammonia solution, making them easier to handle, but may require additional storage considerations. Urea SCR systems utilize urea as a reducing agent, offering safety advantages and lower volatility. Each type of system aims to enhance compliance with environmental regulations while improving air quality.</p></p>
